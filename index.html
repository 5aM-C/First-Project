Our Story

Rooted in Kolhapur,
Made for the World

Our family has been crafting Kolhapuri Chappals, starting as a modest workshop in Kolhapur. What began as a local craft has turned into an act of service — but our methods remain authentic and precise.

Every pair of chappals leaves our workshop having passed through the hands of skilled artisans who have spent years perfecting each cut, stitch, and finish. We use only the finest locally-sourced leather, hand-stitched and polished to a beautiful lustre using our signature polishing process.

Today, our chappals reach customers across India and beyond — worn by people who appreciate genuine craft, cultural heritage, and the kind of quality that only comes from hands that truly care.

The Making

Crafted in
Six Patient Steps

Each pair takes 5–6 days to complete. There are no shortcuts here — only the mastery of generations, done one stitch at a time.

01
🐄

Leather Selection

We source premium buffalo and cowhide leather from trusted local tanneries in Karnataka and Maharashtra — selecting only pieces with the right thickness, grain, and character.

02
💧

Soaking & Softening

The raw leather is soaked in water for hours, making it pliable. Artisans then beat it rhythmically to create the distinctive texture and flexibility that Kolhapuri chappals are known for.

03
✂️

Cutting & Shaping

Using hand-carved wooden lasts and iron tools, each sole and upper is precisely cut. The characteristic Kolhapuri sole shape — slightly wider, rounder — is unique to our tradition.

04
🎨

Color & Finish

We use carefully applied polish to bring out rich, lasting shades in every chappal. This traditional finishing process enhances the natural character of the leather, gives each pair a refined look, and helps the colour mature beautifully with wear.

05
🧵

Hand Stitching

The T-strap, toe loop, and sole are sewn together using thick waxed thread, stitched entirely by hand. Each stitch is tightened by hand — no machine can replicate this tension and care.

06
✨

Finishing & Polish

The chappal is dried in the sun, polished with natural oils, and inspected by the master craftsman. Only pairs that meet our quality standard — the same one since 1962 — leave our workshop.
